Welcome to the World of Ocean Ecosystems

Ocean ecosystems are complex networks of living organisms and their physical environment, playing a crucial role in the health of our planet.

What are Ocean Ecosystems?

Ocean ecosystems can be divided into several types, including coral reefs, estuaries, and open ocean ecosystems, each with its own unique characteristics and inhabitants.

These ecosystems provide a range of ecosystem services, including food, shelter, and breeding grounds for many marine species.

Importance of Ocean Ecosystems

Ocean ecosystems play a critical role in regulating the Earth's climate, providing half of the oxygen we breathe, and serving as a source of food for billions of people.

However, these ecosystems are facing numerous threats, including overfishing, pollution, and climate change, which can have devastating impacts on the health of our planet.

The ocean ecosystem is indispensable for maintaining life on Earth, regulating the climate, providing food, and supporting economic activities. Protecting and conserving ocean health is vital for sustaining biodiversity, ensuring food security, and combating climate change. The future of our planet is closely tied to the health of its oceans.


Human impacts on ocean

Human activities affect ocean ecosystem and ocean habitats through overfishing, habitat loss, the introduction of invasive species, ocean pollution, ocean acidification and ocean warming. These impact marine ecosystems and food webs and may result in consequences as yet unrecognised for the biodiversity and continuation of ocean life forms.
