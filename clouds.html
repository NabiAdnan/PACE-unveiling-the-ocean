Welcome to the World of Clouds

Clouds are collections of tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the air, playing a crucial role in the Earth's climate system.

What are Clouds?

Clouds can be classified into several types, including cirrus, cumulus, stratus, and nimbus, each with its own unique characteristics and formation mechanisms.

Clouds play a critical role in regulating the Earth's temperature, influencing the amount of solar radiation that reaches the surface and the formation of precipitation.

Importance of Clouds

Clouds have a significant impact on the Earth's climate, weather patterns, and ecosystems, influencing the distribution of heat and moisture around the globe.

Clouds also play a critical role in the Earth's water cycle, with precipitation from clouds being the primary source of freshwater for many regions.
