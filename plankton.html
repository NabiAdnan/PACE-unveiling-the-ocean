What is Plankton?

Plankton are tiny plants and animals that drift in the ocean, ranging in size from a few millimeters to several centimeters.

They are the primary producers of the ocean, converting sunlight into energy through photosynthesis.

Importance of Plankton

Plankton are the base of the marine food web, providing food for many marine animals, from small fish to large whales.

They also play a critical role in the ocean's carbon cycle, absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and producing oxygen.
